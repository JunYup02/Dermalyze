Getting startedexpand_more
  1. Pick the body area the lesion is on, front or back.
  2. Upload or photograph a clear, well-lit close-up.
  3. The photo is classified by a Vertex AI image model, and Gemini writes a plain-language summary.
  4. For higher-risk results, Dermalyze can look up nearby dermatology clinics using your device location.
Understanding your resultsexpand_more

Is this a medical diagnosis? No. Dermalyze is an informational AI tool, not a substitute for a licensed dermatologist. Always seek professional care for any concerning lesion.

What do "Texture" and "Pigment" mean? They're short, AI-generated observations about the photo's border/texture and color pattern — grounded in what's visible in your specific image, not a separate diagnostic score.

Why does the analysis sometimes fail? Classification calls a Vertex AI endpoint that has to be deployed and configured by the Dermalyze team. If it isn't connected yet, the upload screen shows a clear notice instead of a broken result.

Privacy and dataexpand_more

Is my photo stored? Your photo is sent directly to the analysis endpoint for classification. It isn't uploaded anywhere else or shown to other users.

Where does "Recent activity" on my dashboard come from? It's saved locally in this browser only, from analyses you've completed here — it doesn't sync across devices.

Account settingsexpand_more

Tap the account icon in the top-right corner of any screen to log out. "Keep me logged in on this device" on the login screen controls whether your session survives closing the browser.
